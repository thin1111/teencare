Bad Habits

  • Avoid Harsh Discipline
  • Blaming Other
  • Procrastination
  • Eating Junk Food
  • Using Drugs
  • Over Thinking

Avoid Harsh Discipline

  • Speaking ill of others reflects on one's own character. Since children learn from their parents, if we engage in bad behavior, children are likely to imitate and follow our actions.

Blaming Other

  • Children learn through what they hear and see. If harsh words are spoken in front of them, they are likely to pick up and imitate such behavior. On the other hand, it can also lead to emotional distress in children.

Procrastination

  • Procrastination transforms a person from being productive to becoming unmotivated and idle. It not only prevents tasks from being completed but also distances one from goals and objectives. Therefore, prevent children from developing the habit of procrastination.

Eating Junk Food

  • Prevent children from consuming junk food. Feeding them junk food close to mealtime can make them prefer junk food over proper meals. Moreover, junk food can lead to clogged arteries and obesity.

Using Drugs

  • Using drugs not only harms your health but also violates the law. Moreover, it sets a negative example for your children

Overthinking

  • Do not let children become overthinkers. Overthinking can harm their physical health and mental well-being.
