How do you Motivate your Teen?

In Order To Prevent

Recently, because parents are becoming busier, there are times when they are unable to give attention to their children. They often get so absorbed in their work, using their valuable time to earn money, that they cannot spend time with their children. Children enjoy spending time with their parents, playing and going places together. Although children want to live happily with their parents, they often suppress their desires when they observe the pressures and struggles of their parents. At first, this may not be an issue, but as time passes, the lack of connection with their parents can lead to feelings of isolation and affect their mental health. Therefore, parents should spend time with their children and create happy memories.

Are you dissatisfied with your teen's lack of work in academics?: 10 Tips to Solve this Problem


Nowadays, most parents are worried since their teenagers are scrolling through social media and playing games a lot.

You can explore more about limiting social media usage for youngsters in Time Limitation.

You may feel frustrated as a parent as your teen is more focused in games rather than academics. We understand how worried parents are for their teenagers' future. That's why we provided this website for parents!

Take a deep breath.




It's quite natural for the guardian to feel worried about their child's future as most jobs are often looked upon your academic achievements.

You want your teenager to be self-motivated so they become a responsible and hardworking adult in the future.

However, most teenagers are already motivated. As for example, if your teenager is interested in any sports which they work hard on: it's already motivation itself.

The motivation that you maybe hoping on is probably school and academics which they are lacking in.

No worries. That's why these tips below are gonna help you motivate your teenager in academic from various methods.



1. Give them more Freedom

Teenagers are trying to find their own way in the current world. Exploring new things, experimenting.

Their desire for independence are everly increasing throughout their teenage years.

Most teenagers have strict rules and schedules which leaves such little room to explore.

It's important to give them some space, so they become more motivated to work harder and more efficiently in their tasks.

Giving them enough freedom can increase their autonomy, making them realise that whatever decision they make with action has consequences as a result. This makes them have a greater sense of control over their lives.

It also boosts your teenager's confidence in achieving any tasks, creation or activity that they are ideal in, as giving them space strengthens their confidence in their decision-making abilities.

Giving your teenager more space to be themselves and make their own decision is a great method for motivation teens!



2. Focus their Progress than their Outcome

Most parents are more proud of their teenagers getting good grades than the actual process.

What truly matters are the skills your teenager learns through the process of the journey like their determination, responsibility and resilience.

Focusing on your teenager's process rather than the outcome will help them develop a strong work ethic and a love for learning.

Celebrate their consistency in studying instead of praising them for getting an A for an exam.

I know that you maybe more proud of your teenager getting an A in an exam. But, what's the point if he gets A in classes that are tend to be easy. The process is more crucial as it relates with your teen's self-esteem.

Congratulate your teen for the immense process they did for their progress instead of their outcome.



3. Listen to them with Curiosity

When their teenagers confronts a problem, parents tend to say "when I was your age" or they compare their teenager with others

Don't Do This.It makes your teenager less likely to to speak with you and ask for advice and help.

Instead of dismissing their problem or immediately providing them with a solution, listen to them more and let them speak. This creates a sense of realisation from your teenager that you're paying attention to them.

This kind of active listening makes your teenager confront more about their problems to you and overall increasing their respect towards you.

You can give guidance to them but best if you ask if they want it first.

If you're teenager doesn't talk much with you then try to make family dinners an option.

Studies shown that having family dinners could boost the relationship and higher possibility of starting a conversation. This makes you get the chance for your teenager to speak to you about their problems.



4. Support their Hobbies

Focusing on academics is essential but so is other activities.

If your teen has interest in any kinds of hobbies like playing soccer, basketball, tennis then that's great!

Hobbies are mostly driven by intrinsic motivation which means teenagers participates in them because they enjoy the activity itself. If you support their hobbies, your teenager is more likely to stay engaged with the activity and overall increasing their motivation.

If they were to succeed in that activity, they'll be more likely to realise that they can achieve anything as long as they're interested in it.

But first, An Important Step In Motivating Your Teen Is Identifying What Actually Motivates them

A great way to find this out is by exploring different kinds of activites with your teen alongside.



5. Be a good Role Model

First and foremost, the primary teachers of children are their parents. Before children even go to school, it is their parents who teach them how to speak, walk, and other fundamental skills. Additionally, the heroes or role models for children are often their parents. Therefore, parents should avoid displaying rough behaviors, using harsh language, or engaging in harmful activities like drinking or drug use in front of their children. Children, who are quick to learn from observation, may think that if their parents behave in these ways, they can do the same. So, if you want your children to become the best they can be, you must avoid such behaviors yourself.

In order for your teen to get motivation in studying. You must give them a good example first.

Take a moment to think about yourself. Do you always clean your room or finish your tasks in time?

This is crucial as teenagers tend to replicate their parents' actions.

I'm not suggesting you to be perfect. However, teenagers watch their parents closely and model their behaviour after them.

Even if it means you have to go down the same path, try to complete short tasks that you could finish within a few minutes such as doing the laundry. Overtime, this makes your teenager replicate you and he/she will have a high tendency to finish their tasks quickly.

Embrace new challenges even if it's sounds scary. This also boost your teenager's confidence in replicating you as the believe they could do it also.



6. Let Natural Consequences Take over them

Just because you don't want to micromanage your teenagers, that doesn't mean they shoudn't suffer the consequences.

Every mistake has consequences which your teen needs to accept and learn from it. Only from then, they'll know what to do when this happens and what not to do when something happens.

For example, if your teen doesn't clean their room, they'll be living in a room filled with trash and difficult to walk around.

Even if your teen fails an exam, resist the urge to make them retake the exam and let them modify their consequences instead. This promotes their motivation to study more efficiently.

As a parent, it's quite natural to save your teen from suffering but this type of problem but it's one of the best ways to enhance their intrinsic motivation and their ability to make wise decisions.



7. Give Positive Feedback - Encouragement

Positive feedback is important for children because it builds their self-esteem and encourages good behavior.

When children are acknowledged for their efforts, they feel valued and motivated to keep making good choices.

It helps them feel closer to their parents and more willing to communicate.

Positive feedback teaches them kindness and respect by setting a good example. It also boosts their confidence and encourages them to keep improving.

Overall, positive feedback helps children grow emotionally and strengthens their relationship with their parents.

9. Get a Mentor

Mentoring is important for kids and teens because it provides support, guidance, and encouragement. It helps them.

They Feel supported during challenges.

They Succeed in school and develop leadership skills.

It can Improve mental health and self-confidence.

They will be able to access new opportunities and resources.

They will Learn from positive role models.

It can build a sense of belonging and self-worth.

As a parent, mentoring can help your child grow, succeed, and feel valued, whether through formal programs or informal relationships.



10. Make them Organise their Task

To help kids stay organized, break tasks into smaller steps and use checklists to keep track of progress

Teach them to use calendars for time management and to create daily routines for structure.

Encourage color-coding for school subjects and memory aids like songs or acronyms to make information easier to remember

Set up a quiet, organized workspace for studying, clean out backpacks regularly, and review plans for the next day to help them feel prepared.

These strategies can reduce stress and help kids stay on top of their tasks.
